About the event

Our next DDaT Together event will be held at the Kia Oval on Wednesday 27 February.

We are fortunate to have two keynote speakers on the day: Mark Thomson, Director General, HM Passport Office, in the morning and Shona Dunn, Second Permanent Secretary, in the afternoon.

This event is open to everyone in DDaT but space is limited so places will be allocated on a first-come-first-served basis.

Don’t forget to bring your Home Office pass. We don’t want you to wear it but you must show it when you arrive at The Kia Oval. If you don’t bring it, you may not be able to take part for security reasons.

Any questions? Please contact the DDaT communications team.

Keynote speakers

Morning

Mark Thomson

Director General, HM Passport Office

Mark Thomson was appointed Director General, HM Passport Office in April 2015 and Director General, UK Visas and Immigration in October 2016. On 24 August 2015, Mark also became Registrar General for England and Wales.

Before joining the Civil Service, Mark had spent his entire career at Royal Mail where he held a variety of Managing Director roles. His early career was in sales and marketing and he also spent several years as Sales & Customer Services Director.

Mark was the commercial lead for the Royal Mail/Post Office separation before taking on the role of Managing Director International in 2011. He has also held several non-executive roles for other organisations including The Chartered Institute of Public Finance and Accountancy (CIPFA) and the former UK Border Agency.

Afternoon

Shona Dunn

Second Permanent Secretary

Shona Dunn joined the Home Office in October 2018.

Before joining the Home Office, she worked at the Cabinet Office where she was Director General of the Economic and Domestic Affairs secretariat.

Shona has held a number of previous senior roles including Director of Fire and Resilience and Director of Town and Country Planning at the Department for Communities and Local Government, as well as Director General of Education Standards at the Department for Education.

Travelling by Tube

It is a 10 minute walk from Vauxhall station to the Oval (Kings Cross/St Pancras or Euston approx 10-12 minutes to Vauxhall station via the Victoria line). The Oval can also be reached via the Northern Line and the main entrance to the ground is 100 metres on your left on leaving the station.

Travelling by Bus

Route numbers 36, 185 and 436 stop outside the ground and other local services stop around the corner. The following buses set down within a 5-minute walk of the ground: 3, 59, 109, 133, 159, 333.
